Bangalore 2-Day Travel Guide

Overview:
- Bengaluru (Bangalore) blends leafy parks, historic sites, cutting-edge tech, craft beer, and some of India’s best South Indian food. Traffic is heavy, so plan clusters by area and use the metro when possible.
- Best for: Food lovers, park enthusiasts, history and architecture buffs, shoppers, craft beer fans, families with kids (museums, parks).
- Core areas to know: Central (MG Road, Cubbon Park, Vidhana Soudha), Basavanagudi/Jayanagar (old Bengaluru charm), Malleshwaram/Rajajinagar (traditional eateries), Indiranagar/Koramangala (cafes, nightlife), Whitefield (tech parks, music venue), Commercial Street/Chickpet (shopping).

Quick-Glance 2-Day Plan:
- Day 1: Classic Bengaluru
  Morning: Lalbagh Botanical Garden walk + traditional breakfast
  Late morning: Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace and/or KR Market
  Afternoon: Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum + Cubbon Park + Vidhana Soudha photo stop
  Evening: Church Street/MG Road (bookstores, coffee) + microbrewery dinner or Karnataka thali
- Day 2: Choose an interest track
  Option A (Heritage + Shopping): Bangalore Palace + Government Museum/Venkatappa Art Gallery + Commercial Street + Indiranagar/Koramangala dinner
  Option B (Temples + Traditional Food): ISKCON Temple (Rajajinagar) + Malleshwaram food trail + Basavanagudi temples (Bull Temple, Dodda Ganapathi) + VV Puram Food Street (evening)
  Option C (Aviation + Live Music): HAL Aerospace Museum + Phoenix Marketcity (shopping) + Windmills Craftworks (jazz/live music, craft beer) in Whitefield
  Option D (Nature + Short Excursion): Nandi Hills sunrise (early start) + relaxed city afternoon (Cubbon Park, bookstores) + dinner in Indiranagar

Detailed Itinerary With Timing and Transport Tips:

Day 1: Parks, Heritage, and Central Bengaluru
- 7:00–9:00 Lalbagh Botanical Garden
  Why: Serene lakes, century-old trees, Glass House, and flower beds. Best in early morning light with fewer crowds.
  Tips: Wear comfortable shoes; carry water. Entry is usually from early morning; small entry fee may apply after early hours.
  Nearby breakfast: 
  - MTR (Lalbagh Road) for dosas, idli-vada, and legendary filter coffee. Expect queues; worth it.
- 9:30–11:00 Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace and/or KR Market
  Tipu’s Palace: Teakwood architecture; quick 30–45 min visit. Modest entry fee.
  KR Market (City Market): Flower market is a riot of color in the morning; watch for crowds and pickpockets. Combine only if you enjoy bustling markets.
  Transport: Both are reachable from Lalbagh by auto or short cab; metro Green Line stations nearby include KR Market and National College.
- 12:00–13:30 Lunch in central area
  Choices:
  - Nagarjuna (Residency Road) for Andhra meals or biryani.
  - Hallimane (Malleshwaram) for homestyle Karnataka fare (requires a short hop north).
  - Airlines Hotel (near MG Road) for classic vegetarian fare in a leafy courtyard.
- 14:00–15:30 Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum (Kasturba Road)
  Why: Hands-on science exhibits; great for all ages. Plan 60–90 minutes. Ticketed.
- 15:30–17:00 Cubbon Park + Vidhana Soudha/High Court
  Stroll or relax in the shade; walk to snapshot the imposing Vidhana Soudha (from outside).
  Metro: Cubbon Park, Vidhana Soudha, and MG Road are on or near the Purple Line corridor.
- 17:00–19:00 MG Road and Church Street
  Do: Coffee at Third Wave/Blue Tokai; browse Blossom Book House or Bookworm for used books; Cauvery Emporium (MG Road) for Karnataka crafts.
- 19:30–22:00 Dinner and drinks
  Microbreweries (choose based on location and vibe):
  - Arbor Brewing Company (near MG Road/Brigade Road): Popular with a central location.
  - Byg Brewski (Sarjapur Road or Hennur): Massive, lively spaces; longer drive.
  - Geist Brewing Co. (Old Madras Road) or URU Brewpark (JP Nagar) as alternatives.
  Non-alcohol option: Classic Karnataka thali at MTR (dinner slots may be limited) or Brahmin’s Coffee Bar (for a lighter, early bite; closes early).
  Transport tip: Evening traffic is dense. Prefer venues close to where you already are or use metro plus a last-mile auto.

Day 2: Pick a Track Based on Interests

Option A: Heritage + Shopping
- 9:00–11:00 Bangalore Palace
  Tudor-style architecture, opulent interiors. Entry fee applies; audio guides available; camera fees may be charged.
- 11:30–13:00 Government Museum and Venkatappa Art Gallery (near Cubbon Park)
  Small but interesting collections of art and archaeology; check current opening hours.
- 13:15–14:30 Lunch
  - Konark (near MG Road) for reliable vegetarian classics.
  - Fatty Bao or Truffles (Indiranagar/Koramangala) for continental/Asian; adapt based on next stop.
- 15:00–17:30 Commercial Street and surroundings
  Bargain for clothes, accessories, and ethnic wear. For sarees and textiles, consider Chickpet (more traditional, very crowded).
- 19:00–22:00 Indiranagar or Koramangala evening
  Cafes, bars, and restaurants line 100 Feet Road and 12th Main (Indiranagar) or 5th Block (Koramangala).
  Dinner picks: Chinita (Mexican), Sly Granny (modern), Meghana Foods (Andhra biryani), or a local microbrewery if you skipped on Day 1.

Option B: Temples + Traditional Food
- 9:00–10:30 ISKCON Temple (Rajajinagar)
  Grand complex; serene morning visit. Dress modestly; remove footwear. Donations optional.
- 11:00–13:30 Malleshwaram food trail
  - CTR (Sri Sagar) for benne masala dosa.
  - Veena Stores for idli-vada.
  - Hallimane for North Karnataka and coastal specials.
  Pick one or two; lines can be long on weekends.
- 14:30–16:00 Basavanagudi temples
  - Bull Temple (Dodda Basavana Gudi) and Dodda Ganapathi Temple.
  Nearby treats: Gandhi Bazaar for snacks and local shopping.
- 19:00–21:00 VV Puram Food Street (Thindi Beedi)
  Street food lane with dosas, bonda, holige, and more. Mostly evening-only; check current status and opening hours. Go hungry and carry small notes/UPI.

Option C: Aviation + Music (Whitefield Side)
- 10:00–12:00 HAL Heritage Centre and Aerospace Museum
  Aircraft displays and aviation exhibits; great for kids. Modest entry fee.
- 12:30–14:00 Lunch near Old Airport Road or Whitefield
  - Windmills Craftworks (Whitefield) for craft beer, food, and jazz vibe (check event schedule).
  - Alternatives: The Whitefield microbrewery scene or mall dining at Phoenix Marketcity/Virginia Mall.
- 14:30–17:00 Shopping or leisure at Phoenix Marketcity (Mahadevpura) or VR Bengaluru
- 19:00–22:00 Live music at Windmills Craftworks (if a show is on) or return to central areas for dinner.

Option D: Nandi Hills Sunrise + Easy City Afternoon
- 4:30–11:00 Nandi Hills excursion
  Leave very early (around 4:30–5:00). Sunrise views are popular; weekends see crowds. Light jacket and shoes recommended. Driving time each way is about 60–90 minutes depending on traffic.
- 12:30–14:00 Return lunch in the city (choose central area).
- 15:00–18:00 Relax in Cubbon Park and browse Church Street/Brigade Road.
- 19:00–22:00 Dinner in Indiranagar/Koramangala.

Food Guide by Area (Pick and Choose):
- Basavanagudi/Jayanagar: Vidyarthi Bhavan (dosa), Brahmin’s Coffee Bar (idli-vada, closes early), Rameshwaram Cafe (popular modern tiffin; expect queues).
- Malleshwaram: CTR/Sri Sagar (benne dosa), Veena Stores (idli), New Krishna Bhavan, Hallimane.
- Central/MG Road/Church Street: Airlines Hotel, Indian Coffee House (nostalgic), Nagarjuna (Andhra), Ebony (views).
- Indiranagar: Modern cafes, Third Wave Coffee, Blue Tokai; global cuisines; many bars and gastropubs.
- Koramangala: Truffles (burgers), Fenny’s, Gilly’s Redefined (live music nights).
- Local specialties to try: Benne dosa, idli-vada with chutney, filter coffee, bisi bele bath, ragi mudde (with saaru), chow chow bath, obbattu/holige, Mysore pak, donne biryani, Andhra meals, Mangalorean seafood (at specialist spots).

Transport and Logistics:
- Airport to city:
  Distance: 35–40 km. Time: 60–120 minutes depending on traffic.
  Vayu Vajra (BMTC) airport buses connect to major hubs (e.g., MG Road, Koramangala, Indiranagar, Whitefield). Tickets on board; frequent services.
  Taxis/app cabs (Ola/Uber) are convenient but can be pricey at peak times.
- Metro (Namma Metro):
  Purple Line (east–west) serves Baiyappanahalli–Whitefield to Kengeri/Challaghatta, including MG Road, Cubbon Park, Vidhana Soudha, Majestic, Lalbagh.
  Green Line (north–south) serves Madavara–Silk Institute, including Yeshwanthpur, Rajajinagar, Majestic, KR Market, Jayanagar, Banashankari.
  Operating roughly from early morning to late evening; buy a smart card for convenience.
  Airport metro line is under construction; plan bus/taxi for airport.
- Buses and autos:
  BMTC buses are extensive; use apps like Google Maps for routes. Autos are plentiful; insist on meter or agree on price before starting. App-based autos available in some apps.
- Cabs:
  Ola/Uber widely available. Expect surge pricing in rain and peak hours.
- Traffic strategy:
  Start early for parks and temples. Cluster sights (e.g., Lalbagh + Basavanagudi; MG Road + Cubbon Park + Vidhana Soudha; Malleshwaram + ISKCON). Use metro for major hops, then autos for last mile.

Costs and Money:
- Entry fees (approximate, subject to change):
  Lalbagh: nominal fee after early hours.
  Tipu Palace: modest fee for Indian nationals; higher for foreign visitors.
  Visvesvaraya Museum: moderate fee.
  HAL Museum: modest fee.
  Bangalore Palace: comparatively higher fee; camera/phone photography charges may apply.
- Meals:
  Traditional breakfast: INR 100–250 per person.
  Mid-range lunch/dinner: INR 300–800 per person.
  Microbreweries: INR 800–1,800 per person depending on drinks.
- Transport:
  Airport bus: INR 200–400 depending on destination.
  Metro fares: INR 10–60 per ride depending on distance.
  Autos/taxis: Variable; check app estimates.
- Payments:
  UPI (digital payments) widely accepted. Cards generally accepted in restaurants and malls; keep some cash for small vendors and food streets.

When to Go and Weather:
- Climate: Generally mild compared to many Indian cities.
  Summer (Mar–May): Warm afternoons (up to low 30s °C), pleasant mornings/evenings.
  Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Frequent showers; carry a rain jacket/umbrella; watch for waterlogging and slow traffic.
  Post-monsoon/Winter (Oct–Feb): Pleasant; cool mornings/evenings; light layer recommended.
- Best times of day: Early mornings for parks and outdoors; afternoons for museums; evenings for dining and walks.

Safety, Etiquette, and Practical Tips:
- Safety: Bengaluru is generally safe. In crowded markets, keep valuables secure. Use registered cabs or reputable apps at night.
- Temples: Dress modestly; remove shoes; photography may be restricted in sanctums.
- Alcohol laws: Bars close earlier than some metros; check closing times (often around 11:30 pm to midnight).
- Language: Kannada is official; English and Hindi are widely understood in tourist and central areas.
- Connectivity: Good 4G/5G coverage; eSIM/SIM easily available. Wi-Fi in most cafes/malls.
- Power: 230V, Type C/D/M plugs.
- Water: Prefer bottled or filtered water. Many cafes provide RO-filtered water.
- Tipping: Not obligatory but appreciated (5–10% in restaurants if service charge not included).
- Dress and comfort: Comfortable walking shoes; light rain protection in monsoon; light layers for cool evenings.

Shopping Notes:
- Commercial Street: Fashion, accessories, footwear, ethnic wear; bargain politely.
- Chickpet: Sarees and wholesale textiles; very crowded; go with time and patience.
- Brigade Road/MG Road: Branded stores, cafes, and some independent boutiques.
- Jayanagar 4th Block: Sarees, fabrics, and local stores in a more relaxed neighborhood setting.
- Crafts: Cauvery Karnataka State Arts & Crafts Emporium for authentic handicrafts and sandalwood items (beware of imitations elsewhere).

With Kids:
- Visvesvaraya Museum, HAL Aerospace Museum, Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium (check show timings), Cubbon Park toy train (operational status varies), Bannerghatta Biological Park (zoo and safari; allow a full half-day).

Day Trip Ideas if You Extend:
- Mysuru (Mysore): Palaces, markets, and gardens; 3–4 hours by road/train.
- Ramanagara: Rock climbing and short hikes; about 1–1.5 hours.
- Channapatna: Traditional wooden toys; combine with Ramanagara.
- Savanadurga: One of the largest monoliths; early start recommended.

Local Metro Stations For Key Sights (for planning):
- Lalbagh Park: Lalbagh station (Green Line) or South End Circle; also accessible by auto from nearby stations.
- Tipu Palace/KR Market: KR Market (Green Line).
- Visvesvaraya Museum/Cubbon Park: Cubbon Park (Purple Line).
- MG Road/Church Street: MG Road or Trinity (Purple Line).
- Vidhana Soudha/High Court: Vidhana Soudha (Purple Line).
- ISKCON Temple: Mahalakshmi or Sandal Soap Factory (Green Line) with short auto.
- Malleshwaram: Mantri Square Sampige Road (Green Line) for quick access to eateries.
- Bangalore Palace: Nearest metro is Cubbon Park or Vasanth Nagar by auto; plan a short cab/auto.

Final Tips to Beat Traffic and Maximize Enjoyment:
- Start early, especially for parks and temples.
- Cluster nearby areas: 
  - Lalbagh + Basavanagudi/Jayanagar
  - MG Road + Cubbon Park + Vidhana Soudha + Church Street
  - Malleshwaram + ISKCON
- Use the metro for long hops and autos for last-mile connections.
- Keep a flexible buffer of 20–30 minutes between activities, more during rain.
- Make dinner reservations for popular spots (especially weekends).

Enjoy your two days in Bengaluru—come hungry, start early, and savor the city’s parks, plates, and pubs.
