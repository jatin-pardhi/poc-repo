A 2‑Day Trip to Africa: A Practical Guide

Africa is a vast, diverse continent with 54 countries, hundreds of languages, and an incredible range of landscapes and cultures. With only 48 hours, the key is focus: pick a single city or nearby area, plan around one or two anchor experiences, and keep logistics simple. This guide helps you choose a destination, prep quickly, and make the most of a short stay with sample itineraries and essential tips.

How to choose where to spend 48 hours
- Think themes, not countries:
  - Iconic city + culture: Cairo, Marrakech, Cape Town, Accra, Dakar.
  - Easy-access wildlife: Nairobi, Johannesburg (day trip to Pilanesberg), Victoria Falls (Livingstone, Zambia or Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe).
  - Beach + old town: Stone Town (Zanzibar), Essaouira, Dakar, Cape Town.
- Consider flight time and arrival hour:
  - From Europe: North and West Africa often have shorter flights. From the Middle East: North/East Africa are well connected. From the US: direct options exist to Accra, Johannesburg, Cape Town (seasonal), and Nairobi; otherwise route via Europe or the Gulf.
- Choose a gateway hub with frequent flights and reliable infrastructure:
  - North Africa: Cairo, Marrakech.
  - East Africa: Nairobi.
  - Southern Africa: Cape Town, Johannesburg.
  - West Africa: Accra, Dakar.

Before you go: fast checklist
- Passport and visas:
  - Check entry rules for your nationality at least 2 weeks ahead. Many countries offer eVisa or visa on arrival (Morocco often visa-free for many, Kenya uses eVisa, Egypt has eVisa).
  - Confirm passport validity (often 6 months) and blank pages.
- Health:
  - Yellow fever: some countries require proof of vaccination, especially if transiting from endemic areas.
  - Malaria: check risk for your destination (e.g., Nairobi city and Cape Town have low risk; coastal or rural areas may require prophylaxis).
  - Bring basics: pain reliever, antihistamine, oral rehydration, hand sanitizer.
- Insurance and safety:
  - Buy travel medical insurance with evacuation coverage.
  - Register emergency contacts and keep local emergency numbers.
- Money and connectivity:
  - Cards are widely accepted in major cities, but carry small cash in local currency for markets and taxis.
  - ATMs available at airports and malls. Notify your bank.
  - Local SIM/eSIM is easy at airports (e.g., Safaricom in Kenya, Maroc Telecom in Morocco, Vodacom in South Africa).
- Packing and power:
  - Light layers, comfortable walking shoes, hat, sunglasses, sunscreen.
  - Adapters vary: North Africa often Type C/E; Kenya Type G; South Africa Type M/N; universal adapter recommended.
- Respect and culture:
  - Dress modestly in conservative areas (shoulders and knees covered at religious sites).
  - Learn a couple of greetings (e.g., “As-salaam alaykum” in North Africa; “Jambo” in Swahili-speaking regions).

How to structure 48 hours
- Anchor experiences: choose one big thing per day (e.g., pyramids, sunrise safari, medina tour, peninsula drive).
- Buffer time: allow 1–2 hours between activities for traffic, queues, and rest.
- Stay central: minimize transit. Book lodging near your primary sights or a transit hub.
- Book in advance: key museum tickets, sunrise/sunset slots, reputable guides, and early-morning game drives.
- Eat local, once fancy, once casual: sample street food and have one sit-down meal with a view.

Four sample 48‑hour itineraries

Cape Town, South Africa
- Why: Mountain-meets-ocean scenery, food and wine, culture, great infrastructure.
- Day 1
  - Morning: Cableway or hike up Table Mountain (go early for calmer winds). Coffee at the top if open.
  - Midday: Explore colorful Bo-Kaap and its museum. Lunch at a Cape Malay kitchen.
  - Afternoon: Stroll the V&A Waterfront; Two Oceans Aquarium if you like.
  - Sunset: Signal Hill or Camps Bay.
  - Dinner: Seafood or a modern South African tasting menu.
- Day 2
  - Morning-to-afternoon: Cape Peninsula loop. Stop at Muizenberg Beach, Boulders Beach penguins, Cape Point, Chapman’s Peak Drive.
  - Late afternoon: Constantia wine tasting or Kirstenbosch Botanical Garden.
  - Dinner: Braai (barbecue) or neighborhood bistro.
- Practical: Uber works well. Weather shifts quickly; pack a windbreaker. Best seasons: spring and fall.

Marrakech, Morocco
- Why: Ancient medina, vibrant souks, desert vibes, photogenic palaces.
- Day 1
  - Morning: Bahia Palace and Saadian Tombs before crowds.
  - Midday: Wander the souks off Jemaa el-Fnaa. Pause for mint tea. Light lunch (tajine or couscous).
  - Afternoon: Ben Youssef Madrasa and a traditional hammam.
  - Sunset: Rooftop terrace overlooking the square’s performers and food stalls.
  - Dinner: Moroccan feast; try pastilla and harira.
- Day 2
  - Morning: Jardin Majorelle and Yves Saint Laurent Museum (book early slots).
  - Midday: Modern cafes in Gueliz or a cooking class.
  - Afternoon: Agafay desert excursion for a sunset camel ride or quad biking (closer than the Sahara).
  - Evening: Return for dinner in the medina.
- Practical: Riads offer charm; arrange airport transfers through your riad. Bargaining is normal in souks.

Nairobi, Kenya
- Why: Urban energy with wildlife on the doorstep.
- Day 1
  - Morning: Giraffe Centre (arrive at opening), then David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust (prebook foster visits if possible).
  - Midday: Karen Blixen Museum or tea farm tour; lunch in Karen.
  - Afternoon: Kazuri bead factory shop and a local craft market.
  - Evening: Nyama choma (grilled meat) dinner.
- Day 2
  - Early morning: Game drive in Nairobi National Park (enter at sunrise for best sightings).
  - Midday: Nairobi National Museum or Railway Museum.
  - Afternoon: Coffee culture in Westlands; pick up souvenirs.
  - Evening: Sundowners with a skyline view.
- Practical: Use reputable taxis or ride-hailing apps (Uber/Bolt). Traffic is heavy; start early.

Cairo, Egypt
- Why: Ancient wonders, lively neighborhoods, Nile sunsets.
- Day 1
  - Morning: Giza Plateau for the pyramids and Sphinx. Hire a licensed guide and start early.
  - Midday: Lunch with pyramid views.
  - Afternoon: Grand Egyptian Museum (if open) or a curated gallery visit; otherwise Coptic Cairo.
  - Sunset: Felucca boat ride on the Nile.
  - Dinner: Koshari or grilled fish.
- Day 2
  - Morning: Egyptian Museum in Tahrir or National Museum of Egyptian Civilization.
  - Midday: Khan el-Khalili bazaar and Al-Azhar area.
  - Afternoon: Islamic Cairo mosques (Sultan Hassan, Al-Rifa’i).
  - Evening: Rooftop cafe overlooking the city lights.
- Practical: Arrange hotel pick-up drivers. Dress modestly for religious sites.

Other great 48‑hour ideas
- Victoria Falls: Stay in Livingstone (Zambia) or Victoria Falls town (Zimbabwe) for viewpoints, helicopter flight, and sunset cruise.
- Zanzibar (Stone Town): Historic alleys, spice tour, and a nearby beach afternoon.
- Accra: Markets, contemporary art spaces, coastal forts, and nightlife.
- Dakar: Island of Gorée, African Renaissance Monument, coastal dining.

Transport and getting around
- Airport transfers: Prebook through your hotel or use official airport taxis. In some cities ride-hailing is reliable (Uber, Bolt, Careem).
- Within city: Walk where practical, but expect heat. Keep valuables secure; use cross-body bags.
- Day trips: For safaris or peninsula drives, book a reputable operator or driver-guide rather than self-driving in a rush.

Costs (very general, per person, per day)
- Budget travelers: 50–100 USD (hostel/guesthouse, local meals, public transport).
- Mid-range: 100–250 USD (3–4 star hotel, mix of local and sit-down meals, some guided tours).
- Splurge: 250–500+ USD (boutique/luxury hotels, private tours, fine dining).
- Big-ticket add-ons: Hot-air balloons, helicopter flights, or private safaris can add 150–500+ USD.

Quick packing list for 48 hours
- Passport, printed confirmations, travel insurance details.
- Universal adapter, battery pack, charging cables.
- Refillable water bottle, sunhat, sunscreen, sunglasses.
- Light layers, compact rain jacket, comfortable shoes.
- Meds: any prescriptions, motion sickness, basic first aid.
- Modest attire for religious sites; scarf/shawl useful.
- Small daypack and a secure wallet or money belt.

Food and drink tips
- Try local staples: tagine and mint tea (Morocco), koshari and ta’ameya (Egypt), nyama choma and ugali (Kenya), Cape Malay curry and braai (South Africa), jollof rice (West Africa).
- Street food is part of the experience; choose busy, well-reviewed stalls. Avoid raw salads if unsure about water.
- Drink bottled or filtered water where advised. Many hotels provide safe water.

Safety and etiquette
- Common-sense precautions: avoid flashy jewelry, use hotel safes, take licensed taxis at night.
- Scams and touts: a polite “no, thank you” works. Agree on prices before rides or tours if no meter.
- Tipping: Often appreciated for guides and drivers (5–10%). Round up small taxi fares.
- Photos: Ask before photographing people, especially in markets and religious areas.

A 48‑hour template you can adapt
- Arrival evening: Hotel check-in, short walk, early dinner, rest.
- Day 1: Early start core experience; midday break indoors; late afternoon neighborhood stroll; sunset viewpoint; local dinner.
- Day 2: Second anchor experience; lunch near next sight; souvenir stop; leisurely finale (boat ride, rooftop, hammam); head to airport.

When to go
- North Africa (Morocco, Egypt): Best Oct–Apr for cooler temps; summers are hot.
- East and Southern Africa: Dry season Jun–Oct is ideal for wildlife; shoulder seasons offer good value.
- West Africa: Weather varies; check rainy seasons and plan for heat and humidity.
- Consider holidays and festivals: Ramadan affects hours and dining in some countries; cultural festivals can be rewarding but busier.

Final quick wins
- Book sunrise or first-entry slots for top attractions.
- Travel hand luggage only if possible.
- Build in a 2–3 hour airport buffer on departure day.
- Keep flexibility for one spontaneous moment—it’s often the highlight.

With a focused plan, even 48 hours can deliver a memorable introduction to Africa’s people, flavors, and landscapes. Pick one place, start early, eat well, and let the continent’s energy do the rest.
