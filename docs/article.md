Here’s a concise, first-timer-friendly, 2-day plan that blends culture, food, and nature around Colombo, with clear options for beaches and big-name day trips. You can mix and match based on your arrival time and energy.

Quick snapshot
- Base: Colombo (easy access to temples, markets, food, beaches, and rail)
- Best time for Colombo/West Coast: December–March (dry, sunny). Shoulder months: November and April. Expect heavier rain May–June and Oct–Nov.
- Currency: Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). As a rough guide, 1 USD ≈ LKR 300–350 in recent years, but rates fluctuate—check current rates.
- Getting around: Tuk-tuks (use a metered ride or the PickMe app), trains for beaches, private car for day trips.

Day 1: Classic Colombo (culture + markets + sunset by the sea)
Morning
- Gangaramaya Temple and Seema Malaka (on Beira Lake)
  - Why go: Iconic Buddhist complex mixing modern and traditional art, with a serene lakeside shrine.
  - Tips: Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered). Shoes and hats off. Early morning is quieter and cooler.
- Colombo National Museum (optional but rewarding)
  - 10–15 minutes by tuk-tuk from Gangaramaya.
  - Offers context on Sri Lankan history, kingdoms, and art.

Lunch: Hoppers and curries
- Try plain hoppers (appa) or egg hoppers with pol sambol (coconut-chili), seeni sambol (sweet onion), and kiri hodi (coconut milk gravy).
- Great casual options: local “hotel” eateries or popular hopper spots in Kollupitiya/Bambalapitiya. Ask for “less spicy” if needed.

Afternoon
- Pettah Market and Jami Ul-Alfar “Red” Mosque
  - Explore spice shops, fabric lanes, and street snacks. Keep valuables close—bustling and busy.
- Independence Square and Arcade Independence
  - Breezy, green, and photogenic; a good breather after Pettah.
- Viharamahadevi Park (optional) for a short walk if you skipped the museum.

Sunset and Street Food
- Galle Face Green
  - Sunset over the Indian Ocean, kite-flying, and classic street snacks.
  - Don’t miss isso wade (prawn fritters), achcharu (pickled fruit), and fresh king coconut water.

Dinner
- Kottu roti (the rhythmic clanking on the grill is half the fun) or seafood.
- Dishes to try: devilled prawns, black pork curry, dhal (parippu), gotukola mallum, lamprais, and curd with treacle for dessert.

Evening option
- Dutch Hospital Precinct (colonial-era complex with restaurants and bars) or a night city view from Colombo Lotus Tower (check hours).

Day 2: Choose your adventure
Option A: Beaches from Colombo (easy, relaxed)
- Morning: Train or tuk-tuk to Mount Lavinia
  - 20–40 minutes by commuter train from Colombo Fort. Swim, sunbathe, or enjoy a beachside brunch.
- Afternoon: Negombo
  - 45–60 minutes drive north from Colombo (closer to the airport).
  - Walk the beach, see the Dutch canal and modest fort ruins, or take a lagoon boat ride (birdlife, mangroves).
- Return to Colombo for dinner (try a Sri Lankan rice and curry spread—rice with multiple curries and sambols).

Option B: Kandy Day Trip (temple, tea, and hills; culture-forward)
- Travel: 3–4 hours each way by train or 3–3.5 hours by car (start early).
- Highlights:
  - Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa): sacred Buddhist site. Dress modestly, remove shoes/hats.
  - Kandy Lake walk and local market.
  - Tea experience: visit a tea factory on the outskirts (Peradeniya side), learn the process, taste.
- Lunch: Rice and curry or a vegetarian buffet; try jackfruit curry and beetroot curry if available.
- Return to Colombo by evening.

Option C: Sigiriya Day Trip (iconic rock fortress; nature-forward)
- Travel: 3.5–4.5 hours one way by car (best with a private driver; depart before dawn).
- Highlights:
  - Sigiriya Rock Fortress: climb early for cooler temps and fewer crowds (frescoes, lion’s paw terrace, summit views).
  - Pair with Dambulla Cave Temple (UNESCO), known for cave murals and Buddha statues.
- Lunch: Local rice-and-curry or string hoppers with sambols in Dambulla or Habarana.
- Return to Colombo late evening.

Must-visit highlights in/near Colombo
- Gangaramaya Temple + Seema Malaka
- Independence Square and its colonial ensemble
- Pettah Market + Red Mosque
- Colombo National Museum (optional but informative)
- Galle Face Green sunset

Food and drink you shouldn’t miss
- Hoppers and egg hoppers with sambols and kiri hodi
- String hoppers (idiyappam) with coconut-based gravies
- Kottu roti (chicken, cheese, egg, or veg)
- Sri Lankan rice and curry (multiple veg + one protein curry)
- Seafood: devilled prawns/cuttlefish, crab at seafood shacks
- Short eats: fish cutlets, rolls, vadai
- Isso wade at Galle Face
- Pol sambol, gotukola mallum, tempered dhal
- Sweet treats: wattalappam (jaggery custard), buffalo curd with kithul treacle
- Drinks: king coconut water, Ceylon tea, Lion Lager, arrack cocktails

Practical tips
- Getting around:
  - Tuk-tuks: Use a meter or agree on the fare before you start. The PickMe app (tuk-tuks and cars) is reliable in Colombo.
  - Trains: Handy for Mount Lavinia and Negombo; buy tickets at Colombo Fort station.
  - Day trips: Hire a private driver for Kandy/Sigiriya if time is tight; group tours also run daily.
- Money:
  - ATMs are common in Colombo; cash is king at small eateries and markets.
  - Keep small notes for tuk-tuks and street food.
- Connectivity:
  - Buy a local SIM at the airport (Dialog, Mobitel) or consider an eSIM. Data is inexpensive.
- Weather and packing:
  - Hot and humid year-round; carry water, sunscreen, a hat, and light clothing.
  - A compact umbrella/rain jacket is useful in monsoon months.
  - Mosquito repellent is advisable.
- Safety:
  - Colombo is generally safe; watch for pickpockets in crowded markets and on trains.
  - Use pedestrian crossings carefully—traffic can be assertive.
  - Swim near lifeguarded zones; currents can be strong.
  - Avoid ice from unknown sources; choose busy food stalls for freshness.
- Airport transfers:
  - Bandaranaike International (CMB) is near Negombo. To Colombo: 45–90 minutes by taxi depending on traffic; rideshare counters exist at arrivals.
- Time zone and power:
  - UTC +5:30. Plug type G (UK-style), 230V.
- Language:
  - Sinhala and Tamil are official; English is widely used in Colombo. A simple “Ayubowan” (hello) and “Istuti” (thank you) go a long way.

Cultural etiquette
- Temples:
  - Dress modestly: shoulders and knees covered; remove shoes and hats; sunglasses off.
  - Do not take photos with your back to Buddha statues; be discreet with photography.
  - Keep voices low; avoid public displays of affection on temple grounds.
- Eating:
  - It’s fine to eat with hands (right hand preferred). Many places provide hand-wash stations.
- Tipping:
  - Restaurants often include a service charge; if not, 5–10% is appreciated.
  - Round up for tuk-tuks and porters.

Budget estimates per person per day (excluding long-haul flights)
Note: Prices vary with season, exchange rate, and your choices. Below are realistic ranges for first-timers.

- Colombo/beaches day
  - Shoestring (excl. hotel): USD 30–50 (LKR equivalent). Breakdown: food 10–15; local transport 5–10; entries/snacks 5–10; extras 5–10.
  - Mid-range (excl. hotel): USD 50–80. Nicer restaurants, a few coffees, museum entry, more tuk-tuks.
  - Hotels: budget guesthouse 25–50; mid-range 60–120; boutique 120–200+.

- Kandy day trip
  - By train/bus + local tuk-tuks (shoestring): USD 40–70 including Temple of the Tooth entry, meals, local transport.
  - Private car/driver (comfortable, fastest): USD 80–150 per person depending on group size (car cost shared).

- Sigiriya + Dambulla day trip
  - Expect higher costs due to distance and entry fees.
  - Private car/driver: USD 120–200 per person depending on group size and ticket prices.
  - Sigiriya and Dambulla have significant foreigner entry fees; bring cash/card as required.

Sample 2-day quick planner
- Day 1: Gangaramaya and Seema Malaka → National Museum (or Viharamahadevi Park) → Lunch: hoppers → Pettah Market + Red Mosque → Independence Square → Galle Face Green sunset and isso wade → Dinner: kottu or seafood at a popular spot.
- Day 2 (choose one):
  - A) Beach combo: Mount Lavinia morning swim → Negombo afternoon lagoon/fort → Dinner back in Colombo.
  - B) Kandy culture: Early train/car → Temple of the Tooth → Lake walk → Tea factory → Return.
  - C) Sigiriya nature: Pre-dawn departure → Climb Sigiriya → Dambulla Cave Temple → Return late.

What to book ahead
- Train seats to Kandy (if you want reserved class); otherwise, go early for unreserved.
- Private driver for Sigiriya or Kandy day trip.
- Popular restaurants at peak times if you prefer specific places.

Final time-savers
- Start early to beat heat and crowds, especially for markets, temples, and Sigiriya.
- Cluster sights by area to minimize tuk-tuk time.
- Keep a scarf or light shawl in your day bag for temple dress codes.

With this plan, you’ll experience Colombo’s spiritual side, colonial and modern history, the island’s beloved street foods, an ocean sunset, and either the tea-scented hills of Kandy or the unforgettable vistas atop Sigiriya.
