A trip to Peru: 2-day Lima and Cusco travel guide

Overview
- This fast-paced 2-day plan splits time between Lima (food, history, culture) and Cusco (Inca heritage and the Sacred Valley). If you’re sensitive to altitude, consider swapping the Sacred Valley day trip for a lighter day in Cusco city.

Day 1: Lima
- Morning – Historic Center
  - Plaza Mayor (Plaza de Armas): See the Cathedral of Lima and Government Palace. Try to catch the changing of the guard (late morning).
  - Monastery of San Francisco and catacombs: One of Lima’s most famous historic attractions.
  - Stroll Jirón de la Unión and nearby colonial-era streets for architecture and street life.
- Lunch – Ceviche and Peruvian cuisine
  - Ceviche is traditionally a midday dish. Look for busy, local cevicherías for the freshest catch.
  - Beyond ceviche: Try causa (potato terrine), anticuchos (grilled beef heart), lomo saltado (stir-fried beef), ají de gallina (creamy chicken), and chifa (Peruvian-Chinese) or Nikkei (Peruvian-Japanese) fusion if you’re curious.
- Afternoon – Larco Museum (Museo Larco)
  - Located in Pueblo Libre, it’s one of Peru’s best museums for pre-Columbian art and ceramics. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  - The museum café is a pleasant spot for a coffee or dessert in the garden.
- Late afternoon to night – Barranco nightlife district
  - Head to Barranco for street art, galleries, and sunset at the Bajada de los Baños and the Bridge of Sighs.
  - Evening options include pisco sours, craft beer (for example, Barranco Beer Company), live music, and peñas (folkloric music venues).
  - If you prefer a calmer evening, walk the seaside Malecon in nearby Miraflores before heading to Barranco.

Getting around Lima (including local transit/colectivos)
- Metropolitano and buses: The Metropolitano BRT is fast along the coast/Miraflores/Barranco corridor. You’ll need a rechargeable card (buy or borrow from your hostel).
- Colectivos: Small, often privately operated shared minibuses or cars running fixed routes. They’re cheap (often under US$1), pay cash to the cobrador (fare collector), and you’ll need to listen for route calls or ask. Keep valuables secure and avoid during rush hour if you dislike crowds.
- Taxis and ride-hailing: Use apps (Beat, Uber, Cabify) for transparency and safety; agree on or confirm the fare before you ride if you hail a taxi on the street.

Day 2: Cusco and the Sacred Valley (or an easier city day if altitude hits)
- Early flight to Cusco
  - Cusco sits at about 3,400 m (11,150 ft). Take it slow on arrival: hydrate, avoid alcohol, and consider coca tea. If you’re prone to altitude sickness, talk to your doctor about acetazolamide (Diamox) in advance.
- Option A – Sacred Valley day trip (group tour or DIY)
  - Typical group tour loop: Pisac (market and hilltop ruins), Ollantaytambo (Inca fortress and cobblestone town), and sometimes Chinchero or a viewpoint en route.
  - Group tours: US$20–35 per person (transport and guide; entrances extra).
  - Entrances: Many sites require a Cusco Tourist Ticket (Boleto Turístico). The full ticket covers multiple sites over several days; partial tickets cover selected circuits and are cheaper. Expect roughly US$20–40 depending on ticket type and currency rates; confirm current prices locally.
  - DIY by colectivos: 
    - Cusco → Pisac: Colectivos from Puputi or Tullumayo streets.
    - Pisac → Urubamba and Urubamba → Ollantaytambo: Frequent colectivos; pay the cobrador in cash.
    - Return Ollantaytambo → Cusco by colectivo or bus. DIY gives flexibility but takes more navigation time.
  - Return to Cusco by evening for dinner and rest.
- Option B – If altitude is challenging, explore Cusco city instead
  - Historic center: Plaza de Armas, Cathedral (and the Church of the Society of Jesus).
  - Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun): Stunning Inca stonework with a colonial church built over it.
  - San Pedro Market: Grab an affordable lunch and fresh juices; great for people-watching.
  - San Blas neighborhood: Artisan shops, cafés, and views; take it slow on the hills.
  - If you’re feeling up for it, a short taxi up to Sacsayhuamán for panoramic views and monumental Inca stonework (ticket required).

Altitude sickness prevention (Cusco and Sacred Valley)
- Hydrate well; avoid alcohol and heavy meals on day one at altitude.
- Take it easy the first 12–24 hours; walk slowly and rest as needed.
- Coca tea and coca candies are commonly used locally for mild symptoms.
- Consider acetazolamide (Diamox) after consulting a doctor; start before ascent if recommended.
- If symptoms worsen (severe headache, nausea, breathlessness), descend or rest; don’t push hard activities.

Budget estimates for backpackers (per person, rough ranges)
- Lima (per day)
  - Dorm bed: US$10–18
  - Meals (budget/local): US$8–15 total if you mix menus del día and a ceviche lunch
  - Local transit: US$2–5 (Metropolitano, buses, colectivos)
  - Museums/sights: US$5–15 (Larco Museum is one of the pricier entries)
  - Nightlife: US$5–15 (drinks, cover charges vary)
- Cusco (per day without Sacred Valley)
  - Dorm bed: US$8–15
  - Meals (budget/local): US$6–12
  - Local transit: US$2–6 (taxis/colectivos around town)
  - Entrances: US$10–20 (depending on which sites you choose)
- Sacred Valley day trip add-on
  - Group tour: US$20–35
  - Entrances (Boleto Turístico or individual sites): roughly US$20–40 depending on ticket choice and currency rates
- Flights and intercity transport
  - Lima ↔ Cusco one-way flight: US$40–120 if booked in advance; more last-minute or in peak season.
- 2-day total estimate (excluding international flights): Approx. US$120–250 depending on tour choices, entrances, and nightlife. You can keep it closer to the lower end by choosing dorms, menus del día, colectivos, and DIY Sacred Valley transport; expect the higher end if you add museums, drinks, and a guided tour.

Practical tips
- Timing: Ceviche is best at lunch; many cevicherías close by late afternoon.
- Safety: Keep phones and wallets secure, especially on public transit and in crowded areas.
- Packing: Layers, a light jacket (cool evenings), sunscreen, hat, and a refillable water bottle. Nights in Cusco can be chilly.
- Cash: Carry small bills/coins for colectivos and markets; ATMs are widely available.
- Flexibility: If the altitude affects you, switch to the easier Cusco city plan and save Sacred Valley for another day.
