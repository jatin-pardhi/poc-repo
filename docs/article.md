Spiti Valley trip plan

1) Quick overview
- Region: Spiti Valley, Himachal Pradesh, India (high-altitude cold desert, 3,000–5,000 m)
- Best windows: mid-May to late June; September to mid-October (clear skies, fewer landslides, open high passes)
- Routes
  - Shimla–Kinnaur–Spiti (NH-5): usually open year-round except during major landslides/snow; slower but better for acclimatization
  - Manali–Atal Tunnel–Kunzum La–Kaza: opens roughly mid-June to mid-October (Kunzum and access to Chandratal depend on snow clearance)
- Trip pattern: Ascend via Shimla side and exit via Manali side to minimize altitude sickness and maximize scenery

2) Road and pass status (to finalize travel dates)
- Check Manali–Kunzum–Kaza status if planning the circuit or Chandratal
  - Kunzum La and Chandratal track usually open mid-June to early October
- Check Kinnaur updates for landslides/closures (Rampur–Jeori–Tapri–Karcham–Reckong Peo–Nako–Tabo)
- How to check
  - Himachal PWD/BRO and local administration advisories
  - HRTC depot numbers (Reckong Peo, Kaza, Manali) for bus operations
  - Local operator/homestay confirmation a week before travel
- Monsoon caution: July–August brings landslide risk in Kinnaur; winter routes can be icy/snowbound

3) Permits and fees
- Indian nationals: No Inner Line Permit required for the standard Spiti circuit (Kaza, Tabo, Dhankar, Langza, Hikkim, Komic, Key, Kibber, Chicham). ID checks occur at checkposts (Sumdo).
- Foreign nationals: Generally no Protected/Restricted Area Permit needed for the core Spiti circuit; certain border roads (e.g., Shipki La, Kaurik) remain restricted/closed. Carry passport/visa. Verify latest advisory before travel.
- Manali side entries:
  - Green Tax/Environment Fee at Manali may apply (pay at barrier or online if available)
  - Rohtang tourist permit is only for visiting Rohtang Top; transit via Atal Tunnel to Lahaul/Spiti typically doesn’t need a Rohtang permit (rules can change—verify on HP e-Permit portal)
- Drones: Often restricted near monasteries and border areas; seek local permission

4) Transportation options
- Self-drive car: Prefer SUV/high ground clearance, good tyres, full-size spare, tyre inflator, tow rope, basic toolkit
- Motorcycle: 200–500 cc bikes commonly used; carry spare tubes, puncture kit, chain lube, rain covers
- Public transport:
  - HRTC buses connect Shimla–Reckong Peo–Kaza; and (seasonally) Manali–Kaza
  - Shared cabs operate between major villages; flexible but slower
- Driver hire/taxi: Daily rates vary by season and terrain; negotiate including driver stay/meals if needed
- Fuel: Pumps at Tapri, Reckong Peo/Pooh side, and Kaza (keep topped up; carry spare if riding)

5) Connectivity, cash, and services
- Mobile/data: BSNL/MTNL most reliable; Jio 4G present in/around Kaza; Airtel/Vodafone limited. Expect blackouts in remote villages.
- ATMs and payments: SBI ATM in Kaza; card/UPI acceptance improving but spotty. Carry sufficient cash.
- Medical: Community Health Centre in Kaza; basic pharmacies in Kaza/Tabo. Evacuation can be slow.

6) Altitude and acclimatization
- Ascend gradually via Shimla side if possible
- Rest day in Kaza before high villages (Langza/Hikkim/Komic)
- Hydrate, avoid alcohol/smoking early on, eat light and frequent meals
- AMS awareness: headache, nausea, dizziness, fatigue, sleep disturbance
- Medication: Acetazolamide (Diamox) 125–250 mg once/twice daily can be considered 12–24 hours before sleeping above 3,000 m; consult your doctor first
- Oxygen cans provide brief relief only; seek descent/medical help if symptoms worsen

7) Sample itineraries

Option A: 9 days (classic circuit via Shimla in, Manali out; good acclimatization)
- Day 1: Shimla or Narkanda night halt
- Day 2: Narkanda → Sangla/Chitkul or Kalpa (Reckong Peo) night halt
- Day 3: Kalpa → Nako → Tabo night halt (visit Tabo Monastery if time)
- Day 4: Tabo → Dhankar (short hike to Dhankar Lake if acclimatized) → Kaza night halt
- Day 5: Kaza acclimatization/rest; local Kaza market, Key Monastery (optional easy day)
- Day 6: Kaza → Langza → Hikkim (post office) → Komic → back to Kaza
- Day 7: Kaza → Kibber → Chicham Bridge → Losar (if Manali road open, continue) → Chandratal camps (seasonal) night halt
- Day 8: Chandratal → Kunzum La → Atal Tunnel → Manali night halt
- Day 9: Manali → home/next destination

Option B: 7 days (Shimla side in and out when Manali–Kunzum is closed)
- Day 1: Shimla/Narkanda
- Day 2: Kalpa
- Day 3: Kalpa → Nako → Tabo
- Day 4: Tabo → Dhankar → Kaza
- Day 5: Langza–Hikkim–Komic circuit; back to Kaza
- Day 6: Key–Kibber–Chicham; back to Kaza or Tabo
- Day 7: Return via Kalpa/Rampur; buffer in case of delays

Option C: 12 days (slow travel)
- Add extra night in Tabo or Dhankar for monastery stays
- Add Pin Valley (Mud village) if road open
- Add second night in Kaza and one more at Chandratal (weather permitting)
- Include short hikes (Langza ridgelines, Kibber–Chicham area)

8) Accommodation suggestions (generic)
- Kaza: Homestays/guesthouses; budget hostels and mid-range hotels available
- Tabo: Monastery guesthouse, local homestays
- Dhankar: Village homestays (limited, book ahead); stunning sunset views
- Kalpa/Sangla/Chitkul: Ample guesthouses and homestays
- Chandratal: Seasonal tented camps (June–Sept), no permanent structures near the lake; book ahead
- Tips: Choose homestays to support locals; confirm heating/water availability; ask about meal options (simple, hearty, vegetarian often available)

9) Packing list (essentials)
- Clothing (layering system)
  - 2–3 moisture-wicking base layers (top/bottom)
  - 1–2 mid layers (fleece/wool)
  - Insulation jacket (down/synthetic)
  - Waterproof/windproof shell (jacket and pants if possible)
  - Warm hat/beanie, buff/neck gaiter
  - Gloves (warm + waterproof shell if riding)
  - Trekking shoes with good grip; spare socks (wool/synthetic)
- Sun and skin protection
  - Sunglasses with UV protection (cat 3/4 ideal)
  - Sunscreen SPF 50+, lip balm with SPF
  - Moisturizer, hand cream
- Health and safety
  - Personal meds; basic first aid (painkillers, antiemetic, anti-diarrheal, bandages, blister care)
  - Diamox (if doctor-advised), ORS sachets
  - Headlamp with spare batteries
  - Refillable water bottle/hydration bladder; thermos in colder months
- Travel and gear
  - ID proofs, permits/receipts, copies (paper + digital)
  - Cash reserve; cards; UPI if available
  - Phone + power bank(s), cables, car charger
  - Offline maps downloaded (Google/Maps.me)
  - Small daypack, rain covers
  - Snacks (dry fruits, energy bars), thermals for sleep
- Vehicle (if self-drive/ride)
  - Full-size spare, inflator, puncture kit, tow rope, jump leads
  - Coolant, engine oil top-up, basic tools, funnel, spare fuses
  - Jerry can (if riding long stretches)
- Optional winter add-ons (Nov–Mar)
  - Heavier insulation, hand/foot warmers, microspikes for ice

10) Budget estimation (per person; rough guidance)
- Transportation
  - Fuel (complete circuit ~1,000–1,400 km depending on start point): estimate based on vehicle mileage and current fuel prices
  - Bike rental: ~INR 1,200–1,800 per day (model dependent, security deposit extra)
  - Taxi hire: ~INR 4,000–7,000 per day for SUV with driver (season, negotiation, route)
  - HRTC buses: budget-friendly; multi-day hops add up to ~INR 2,000–4,000 total
- Stay: INR 800–2,500 per person per night (homestay/guesthouse range)
- Meals: INR 500–900 per person per day (simple veg, tea/coffee)
- Fees: Manali green tax; monastery donations; parking; occasional camera fees
- Chandratal camp: INR 1,200–3,500 per person per night with meals (seasonal)
- Example 9-day circuit (mid-range, shared room, 2–3 people sharing car)
  - Transport (fuel split or taxi): INR 12,000–22,000 per person
  - Stay (9 nights avg INR 1,200): INR 10,800
  - Food (9 days avg INR 700): INR 6,300
  - Misc (fees, tips, permits, buffer): INR 2,000–4,000
  - Total ballpark: INR 31,000–43,000 per person
- Solo on public transport/hostels can be done under INR 25,000–30,000; more comfort or private taxi can exceed INR 50,000

11) Day-by-day booking checklist
- Before booking dates
  - Confirm Kunzum/Chandratal status if planning Manali exit and lake stay
  - Check Kinnaur landslide advisories for NH-5
  - Decide route and duration (7, 9, or 12-day pattern)
- Transport bookings
  - Self-drive: service vehicle, tyres, spares; arrange FASTag and PUC; print insurance
  - Bike rental: reserve in advance (Shimla/Manali); verify papers, test ride, toolkit
  - HRTC: check latest timetables; arrive early for seat; consider buffer days
- Accommodation bookings
  - Lock first and last night (Shimla/Narkanda, Manali) early in season
  - Book Kaza (2–3 nights), Tabo/Dhankar (1 night), Chandratal camp (seasonal)
  - Keep one buffer night unbooked for contingencies
- Permits/fees
  - Pay Manali green tax if entering via Manali
  - Carry multiple ID copies for checkposts; foreigners carry passport/visa
- Health prep
  - Doctor consult for high altitude travel; discuss Diamox and any conditions
  - Pack first aid and personal meds; consider travel insurance covering high altitude and road evacuation
- Final confirmations (T-3 to T-1 days)
  - Reconfirm road/opening status with homestay or taxi driver
  - Download offline maps, save emergency contacts, share itinerary with a friend/family

12) Driving and safety tips
- Start early each day to avoid afternoon weather and to buffer delays
- Expect rough patches, water crossings (post-snowmelt), and rockfall zones; drive slow and steady
- Yield to uphill traffic; use low gear on descents; avoid tailgating near cliffs
- Never drive/ride at night in the mountains unless unavoidable
- Keep snacks and warm clothes accessible, not buried in luggage
- Respect local customs; seek permission before photographing people or inside monasteries
- Leave no trace: carry your trash until proper disposal; avoid plastic where possible

13) Emergency and important contacts (fill in locally)
- Police/ambulance: 112/108 (state emergency)
- Kaza CHC (hospital): add number after booking
- Local homestay numbers and taxi driver contact: add to phone and written note
- Breakdown support: manufacturer RSA/towing if available; keep helpline handy

14) Notes for winter Spiti (if considering)
- Expect extreme cold (often below -15°C), limited water supply, short daylight, icy roads
- Circuit usually not possible; plan in-and-out via Shimla side only
- No Chandratal in winter; focus on Kaza + nearby villages and monastery stays
- Only travel with experienced local drivers; gear up for sub-zero conditions
